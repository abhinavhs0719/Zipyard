    How this actually works

    1. 01

      Your files stay on your machine

      The browser reads the bytes locally. There is no upload step, so there's nothing to wait on and nothing sitting on a server afterward.

    2. 02

      The archive is built in memory

      A compression library running inside the page assembles the .zip, folder structure included, entirely on your device's own processor.

    3. 03

      You get a normal download

      When it's done, your browser offers the .zip like any other download. Close the tab and everything involved is gone.

    Zipyard runs entirely client-side. The counters above reset with a new day and live only in this browser.
